Project - Planning applications

Progress and timeline

The data design team has been working on behalf of the development management policy team to develop open data specifications for planning application submissions. It is the process that they own which is being adapted to ensure consistent, interoperable data is flowing through it.

We are now developing a timeline to outline the next steps, both towards the mandating of planning application submission data specifications and the development of further specifications for the planning permission process.

2025/26 milestones

Getting the specifications right

We will be:

  • analysing and categorising feedback (now, future, general)
  • iterating the draft specifications where appropriate based on that feedback
  • developing a complementary set of specifications for planning applications and decisions (the open output of the process)
  • working with the development management policy team to gather their feedback
  • working with the development management policy team to iterate specifications where appropriate
  • publishing and publicising codelist datasets
  • developing and sharing v0.1 Declarative Model for pilot specifications
  • seek feedback and iterate v0.1 Declarative Model for pilot specifications (community feedback)
  • developing a test suite (a way of automatically verifying that data follows the expected shape and rules that have been agreed) for testing conformance with the specifications
  • deconstructing the CIL form (covers floorspace, similar to application submissions)
  • supporting DM policy in codifying their existing form update process
  • working with DM policy to re-engineer their update process to start with data specification
  • developing a change process and changelog for the data specifications
  • supporting development management policy in embedding data specifications into their processes
  • drafting and consulting the community on useful codelists (e.g. planning application types)
  • developing an open process for community-initiated specifications to develop and sit alongside the MHCLG specifications
  • restarting the community engagement activities (ie advisory group / drop ins) around planning data specifications with a revised charter to cover our 2025/26 milestones

Supporting the mandating process

We will be:

  • supporting the digital and development management policy teams in developing a consultation pack on new statutory instruments to mandate data specifications as ‘approved data standards’
  • supporting the development management team in identifying how ‘approved data standards’ will work with existing development management legislation (development management procedure order)
  • supporting formal consultation on pilot specifications with community stakeholders
  • moving the specifications from ‘pilot’ to ‘candidate’ as per the data design process

Progress

Data design work to date has been focused on delivering to original deadlines around submission data (committed to in October 2024).

These were the milestones achieved.

Community engagement

We:

  • created an advisory group to support the development of draft baseline specifications based on an application process
  • began wide, open community engagement to support the development of draft baseline specifications
  • set up specific engagement with the development management policy team to make sure the approach was endorsed
  • set up a webpage to share progress

Baseline existing process

We:

  • are analysing existing planning permission forms to identify data modules and fields
  • created a module tracker spreadsheet for community comments

Drafting specifications

We:

  • created individual/modular working draft data specifications based on this analysis and input from the community
  • created composite specifications (ie made up of the modules) to mirror the existing system
  • set up feedback loops for community engagement
  • gathered feedback on modular specifications from the community (deadline for input was 16 May)
